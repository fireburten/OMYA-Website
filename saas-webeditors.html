Introducing Jack's Grapes: a Web Editing App

The Problem

When I decided I wanted to make a full time job hobby out of fiddling around with technology, I figured I should probably get a website to start earning those fake internet points.

URL in hand, I immediately looked up my old stalwart - my High School Bestie: Dreamweaver. Turns out thats now $70.... a month! Tumbling down the rabbit hole, I found plenty of great (and not so great) web editors.  Anything worth using let me "Start for Free!". Turns out that means letting me build just enough before it accosts me to subscribe to the service.

Do we really NEED a another subscription just to put together a basic webpage?! Literally something Thomas's Myspace Engine let us do easily 20 years ago. 

I say no! I'll make my own web editor with "Black Jack and..." plenty of feature functionality. 

The Approach

  • Leverage open source libraries like GrapesJS, NodeJS, NPM to build an easy to use, no nonsense Web Development App
    • Configure and deploy this as a webapp experience
  • Finally build this website!

What I Learned

  • The 2020's change the landscape of Web Development. Everything became and subscription and its harder to build a webpage than it was 10 years ago.
  • When building, get comfortable with squashing bugs. For all the great things AI can help with, it can sometimes introduce more problems than solutions.
  • With great open source communities, AI coding and a little knowledge we are not limited by what we find on Google. The "Make vs Buy" debate now has a very large finger on the scale. Go be creative!
